# The Stock Quality Gate

A stock must pass **≥8 of 10** to be buyable. Two of them (marked HARD) are non-negotiable — fail either and the stock is out regardless of the rest.

| # | Criterion | Threshold | Why it exists |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Market cap | Small/mid band (e.g. ₹2,000–20,000 Cr) | Big enough to survive, small enough to re-rate |
| 2 | Profit growth | PAT CAGR ≥25% — 3yr AND TTM **AND forward** | Growth must be current and continuing, not just historical |
| 3 | ROCE | ≥20% | Quality of the business machine |
| 4 | ROE | ≥15% | Returns actually reaching shareholders |
| 5 | Leverage | D/E < 0.5 (prefer debt-free) | Survives downturns and rate cycles |
| 6 | **Promoter (HARD)** | ≥50% holding, stable, **ZERO pledge** | Skin in the game; pledges are forced-selling bombs |
| 7 | **Cash conversion (HARD)** | Cumulative 5yr CFO/PAT ≥ 70% | See below — this kills most traps |
| 8 | Valuation vs growth | PEG < 1.3 | Never pay 60x for 10% growth |
| 9 | Entry price | ≥10% below 52-week high | Margin of safety; never buy the euphoria |
| 10 | Tailwind | A real multi-year structural driver | Something must power the re-rating |

## The cash-conversion gate (criterion 7) — read this twice

**CFO/PAT = cumulative operating cash flow ÷ cumulative reported profit, over 5 years.**

A company can report years of beautiful profit growth while generating little or no actual cash — the "profit" piles up as unpaid receivables and unsold inventory. These stocks look cheap on PE, screen well on growth, and blow up on one bad cycle.

Red-flag patterns to check on every candidate:
- CFO negative in any 2 of the last 5 years → almost always reject
- CFO/PAT below 0.5 cumulative → reject
- Receivables or inventory growing much faster than sales → investigate before anything else
- Repeated fundraising (QIPs, warrants) while claiming strong profits → where's the cash then?

## The two-step rule: the screen LIES half the time

Every stock that passes the gate gets a **verification pass** before any buy:

1. **Community forum check** (in India: ValuePickr). Extract (a) the current bear case, and (b) whether the thread has gone quiet — informed investors losing interest is itself a signal.
2. **Latest earnings-call check.** The single most common trap: trailing growth that came from ONE unusual quarter (forex gain, inventory gain, one-time order, acquisition). Compare management's own forward guidance to the trailing number. If guidance implies half the trailing growth rate, re-price the stock on guidance.

Known trap taxonomy (all found in real screens):
| Trap | Signature | Catch it by |
|---|---|---|
| Cash-conversion trap | High PAT growth, negative CFO | Criterion 7 |
| One-quarter spike | Flat quarters, then one monster quarter drives "TTM growth" | Read the quarterly line, not the annual |
| Inorganic growth | "+50% growth" that's mostly an acquisition | Concall: organic vs acquired split |
| Cyclical peak earnings | Commodity/gold/freight windfall makes PE look cheap | Management's own next-year guidance |
| Pledge bomb | Great numbers, promoter pledge rising | Criterion 6, checked every quarter |
| Stale smart-money story | "Famous investor buys!" article recycling old data | Verify the disclosure DATE; check who SOLD the same block |
| Guidance serial-misser | Every year's story is next year's disappointment | Forum history + past guidance vs delivery |

## The macro gate (before deploying, not before researching)

Check before any tranche goes out:
- Index vs its trend / a defined "green-light" level
- Volatility index (calm vs fearful)
- Foreign + domestic institutional flows
- Small/mid-cap valuations vs their own history (a 40-50% premium = stagger harder)
- Binary events in the next 2-3 weeks (central bank, earnings season opener, geopolitical deadlines)

Rich valuations + imminent binaries = deploy a small starter at most, keep the rest in defined triggers.

## Position rules
- Staggered entries: day-one deployment is typically 10-25% of the intended allocation; the rest waits for price dips or proof (results).
- Every buy = LIMIT order + stop-loss defined the same day.
- Triggers are levels where you buy *if the level holds* — not falling knives.
- If a trigger never comes, the money stays cash. **Never loosen an entry to force deployment.**
